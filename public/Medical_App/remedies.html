Basic Remedies

Simple first-aid and self-care guidance while waiting for consultation

Emergency Warning

This page is not a substitute for medical advice. Seek emergency help immediately for chest pain, trouble breathing, stroke symptoms, fainting, severe bleeding, severe burns, or rapidly worsening symptoms.

Fever

Rest, drink fluids, and use a cool cloth if uncomfortable.

Seek medical help if fever is very high, lasts long, or comes with confusion, breathing trouble, stiff neck, or dehydration.

Minor Cuts

Wash hands, rinse the cut with clean water, apply gentle pressure if bleeding, then cover with a clean bandage.

Get care if the wound is deep, dirty, infected, or bleeding does not stop.

Minor Burns

Cool the burn under cool running water, remove tight items near the area, and cover with a clean dressing.

Do not apply ice directly. Seek urgent help for large, deep, chemical, electrical, or face/hand/genital burns.

Sprain or Strain

Rest the area, use ice wrapped in cloth, compress lightly, and elevate the limb.

Get care if there is severe pain, deformity, numbness, or inability to bear weight.

Sore Throat

Drink warm fluids, rest your voice, and gargle with warm salt water if suitable.

Seek care if there is breathing difficulty, drooling, severe pain, rash, or symptoms lasting several days.

Dehydration

Sip water or oral rehydration solution slowly and rest in a cool place.

Seek urgent care for confusion, fainting, very little urination, persistent vomiting, or severe weakness.
